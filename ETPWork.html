📘 Which AI Model Rules?

Comparing ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot on English Test Analysis

How do you prepare for an English test? My method is simple: paste the poem’s heading into a chatbot, get the fancy words and descriptions, and reap the grades.

This approach increased my scores by 7%. But which chatbot should you trust?

Here I compare ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot using the same prompt, so we can finally answer: Which AI model does it best?

🧪 The Prompt

“I want to talk about the poem Rain on the Roof by Coates Kinney. Tell me about:
  1. The themes of the poem
  2. Significant uses of the different parts of speech (with examples)
  3. What the poem talks about in: a. First paragraph, b. Second paragraph, c. Third paragraph
  4. Explain the complicated lines:
    • "Every tinkle on the shingles has an echo in the heart"
    • "A thousand dreamy fancies are busy into being start"
    • "Weave their air threads into woof as I listen to the patter of the rain overhead"
    • "To regard the darling dreamers ere she left them till the dawn"

🤖 ChatGPT's Response

1. Themes of the Poem
• Nostalgia and Childhood Memories: The rain reminds the poet of his mother and the warmth of his home during childhood.
• Nature and Emotion: The natural sound of rain evokes deep emotional responses and internal reflections.
• Dreams and Imagination: The soothing rain triggers dreams and fanciful thoughts, almost like a lullaby.
• Love and Loss: The mention of the poet’s mother, now no more, adds a tone of mourning and longing.

2. Use of Parts of Speech (with examples)
Nouns: Shingles, patter, heart, dreamers, dawn
Example: "Every tinkle on the shingles..." – tinkle and shingles are concrete nouns.
Verbs: Fall, start, regard, weave, listen
Example: "...fancies are busy into being start." – start shows the action of beginning.
Adjectives: Melancholy, dear, darling, dreamy
Example: "A thousand dreamy fancies..." – dreamy modifies fancies, suggesting imagination.
Adverbs: Softly, overhead
Example: "...as I listen to the patter of the rain overhead." – overhead tells where the patter happens.
Prepositions: On, into, over
Example: "on the shingles", "into being start"
Conjunctions: As, and, while
Example: "As I listen to the patter..." – As introduces a time clause.
Pronouns: I, she, them
Example: "She used to regard the darling dreamers..."

3. What the Poem Talks About
a. First Stanza: The poet describes the joy of lying on his bed in a cozy room while listening to the rain falling on the roof. It introduces the emotional and calming effect the sound has on him.
b. Second Stanza: The sound of rain awakens the poet’s imagination. He starts to daydream and recall fond memories. The rain inspires a mental world filled with past images and emotions.
c. Third Stanza: The poet specifically recalls his mother, who would lovingly watch over her children as they slept. Her memory returns with the rain, evoking a sense of love and loss.

4. Explanation of Complicated Lines
a. "Every tinkle on the shingles has an echo in the heart"
Meaning: Every small raindrop hitting the roof creates a sound that resonates emotionally with the poet. The sound stirs sentimental reactions.
b. "A thousand dreamy fancies into busy being start"
Meaning: The sound of the rain triggers countless dreams and imaginary thoughts in the poet’s mind.
c. "Weave their air threads into woof as I listen to the patter of the rain overhead"
Meaning: The poet compares his thoughts to threads being woven into a tapestry, suggesting the rich emotional world forming as he listens to the rain.
d. "To regard the darling dreamers ere she left them till the dawn"
Meaning: His mother used to lovingly watch over her children (the “darling dreamers”) before leaving them to sleep, staying with them till dawn.

🔍 Google Gemini's Response

1. Themes of the Poem
• Nostalgia and Memory: The speaker reflects on his childhood and his mother. The rain serves as a powerful trigger for these intimate recollections.
• The Comfort of Nature: The rain is portrayed not as disruptive, but as soothing and peaceful, creating a comforting atmosphere for reflection.
• The Power of Sound: The consistent patter of rain evokes emotions and memories, demonstrating how sound can impact the heart and mind.
• Loss and Longing: There is a subtle sense of yearning for times gone by, especially for the comforting presence of the poet’s mother.
• The Subconscious and Dreams: The rain blurs the boundary between waking life and dream, filling the poet’s mind with “dreamy fancies” and reflections.

2. Significant Uses of Different Parts of Speech
Nouns: Shadows, humid, starlight, cottage-chamber, patter, echo, fancies, woof, darling dreamers
Example: “Every tinkle on the shingles…” – tinkle and shingles highlight sound and setting.
Verbs: Hover, weep, lie, press, listen, tinkle, start, weave, regard, left
Example: “Listen” shows the speaker’s focused attention on the rain, making it the poem’s central action.
Adjectives: Humid, melancholy, starry, tiny, busy, air, darling, fond, refrain
Example: “Melancholy darkness” sets a contemplative and emotional tone for the poem.
Adverbs: Softly, gently
Example: “Softly pressed my pillow” – conveys tenderness and comfort.

3. What the Poem Talks About
a. First Paragraph: Sets the mood with dark, humid skies and personified rain gently “weeping.” The speaker lies in his cozy cottage bed, comforted by the sound of the rain overhead. This evokes a sense of warmth, safety, and reflective calmness.
b. Second Paragraph: The rain stimulates the speaker’s imagination. Each “tinkle on the shingles” sparks “dreamy fancies” and “recollections” that weave together in his mind. The rain serves as a direct trigger for memory and imagination.
c. Third Paragraph: A specific memory emerges—that of his mother lovingly watching over her children as they slept. Her affectionate presence is recalled as he listens to the rain. The “rain’s refrain” becomes a link to this cherished, now distant, moment of care and warmth.

4. Explanation of Complicated Lines
a. "Every tinkle on the shingles has an echo in the heart"
Each raindrop on the roof stirs an emotional reaction within the speaker. The external sound triggers deep, internal feelings and memories, resonating in his heart like an echo.

b. "A thousand dreamy fancies are busy into being start"
The rain doesn’t just evoke passive thoughts; it actively sparks imaginative ideas and memories. The speaker’s mind becomes lively and filled with newly forming dreams.

c. "Weave their air threads into woof as I listen to the patter of the rain overhead"
This metaphor describes how intangible thoughts (“air threads”) are interlaced into a tapestry (“woof”) of memories and dreams. The rain acts as the loom, guiding the formation of this internal mental fabric.

d. "To regard the darling dreamers ere she left them till the dawn"
The speaker’s mother used to lovingly watch her children sleep before quietly leaving them for the night. It reflects maternal tenderness and highlights the speaker’s emotional attachment to that comforting presence, now gone but vivid in memory.

🛠️ Microsoft Copilot's Response

🌧️ 1. Themes of the Poem
• Nostalgia: The rain reminds the poet of his childhood and his mother’s tender presence.
• Comfort and Solace: The rain’s gentle rhythm provides emotional peace and a sense of safety.
• Power of Nature: Rain is shown as a natural force capable of stirring memories and emotions.
• Imagination and Dreams: Rain stimulates the poet’s creativity and reflections, leading to rich daydreams and recollections.

📝 2. Use of Parts of Speech (with Examples)
Nouns: Shingles, pillow, dreamers – Evoke vivid imagery of comfort and memory.
Verbs: Hover, weeps, weave – Bring motion and emotion to the poem’s atmosphere.
Adjectives: Melancholy, dreamy, darling – Set the mood and tone.
Adverbs: Gently, overhead – Describe how actions (like pressing the pillow or the rain falling) occur.
Alliteration: “Humid shadows hover,” “darling dreamers” – Create musical rhythm and emphasis.
Onomatopoeia: Patter, tinkle – Mimic the soothing sounds of falling rain.
Personification: “Melancholy darkness weeps” – Gives human emotions to the night and rain.
Metaphor: “Air-threads into woof” – Compares memories and dreams to woven fabric, creating a deep poetic image.

📜 3. What Each Paragraph (Stanza) Talks About
a. First Stanza: The poet sets a cozy nighttime scene with dark, rainy skies. Lying in his bed, he finds comfort in the sound of the rain, which is personified as softly crying. The mood is introspective and peaceful.
b. Second Stanza: As he listens, each raindrop triggers a wave of memories and dreams. His mind becomes busy with vivid “dreamy fancies” that interweave like threads in a tapestry.
c. Third Stanza: The memory of his mother returns. He recalls how she would lovingly look at her sleeping children before leaving them until morning. The rain’s rhythm strengthens this emotional, nostalgic recollection.

🔍 4. Explanation of Complicated Lines
a. “Every tinkle on the shingles has an echo in the heart”
The sound of rain touching the roof resonates emotionally within the poet, evoking deep personal feelings.

b. “A thousand dreamy fancies into busy being start”
The poet’s imagination is awakened by the rain, filling his mind with vivid and active daydreams and recollections.

c. “Weave their air-threads into woof as I listen to the patter of the rain overhead”
Memories and dreams are likened to threads of air being woven into fabric. The sound of the rain helps the poet interlace these thoughts into a mental tapestry.

d. “To regard the darling dreamers ere she left them till the dawn”
The poet recalls his mother lovingly watching her children sleep before quietly leaving them for the night. It conveys deep affection and nurturing warmth.
